# Thoughts on AI Trends and Their Impact on Society

### AI Trends:

If we look at problems from a developmental perspective, the future trends of AI will include the following aspects.

1. As software and hardware continue to improve, and as electricity costs decline, the cost of intelligence will become lower and lower, while intelligence itself will become stronger and stronger. Compared with two years ago, current models not only cost only one tenth as much to use, but also possess stronger intelligence.

2. AI will become more autonomous, and the degree of human intervention required will become lower and lower. Active AI can release more capability than passive AI. The key lies in how to supervise and prevent problems.

3. Continuous learning ability is one of the key breakthrough capabilities. AI that can learn in real time based on environmental feedback will become stronger and stronger. After learning, it can also synchronize with other AIs, sharing one soul. Any single AI agent will then comprehensively surpass humans.

4. Model routing: assigning different models according to different needs. Simple tasks use lighter-weight AI, while complex tasks use more intelligent AI. Tasks that depend on fast response will be placed on the edge side, while scenarios with low latency requirements but stronger intelligence demands will be placed in the cloud. At the same time, communication and coordination between cloud-side AI and edge-side AI will become more and more common. For example, a main cloud AI may assign tasks to many edge-side sub-AIs.

5. AI will become the new traffic entry point. In the future, search will basically be done through AI, and only when opening a certain official webpage will people use a browser. Later, AI output will not be limited to text, but will also expand to images or video. Right now, when we use AI, we ask AI first whenever we have any question, instead of going to a browser to search for the answer, whether it is about emotions, work, life, or knowledge. In the future, advertising based on the AI entry point will be the most precise form of advertising. The context of your conversation with AI is the most precise label, and AI knows extremely well what you need. To-C monopoly model companies will become the companies with the highest market value. The reason is that, in the future, the AI window will replace phones and computers as the all-purpose entry point, because the ideal interaction method is simply that you give a task and AI completes it automatically. OpenAI will definitely make use of its huge user-base ecosystem to replace Facebook, replace Google Search, replace Apple, replace Amazon, and replace electronic payment channels. It is just that companies that already have ecosystem barriers will be replaced more slowly, while those without ecosystem barriers will become merchants that pay advertising fees to OpenAI and rely on OpenAI, such as Uber. Whichever company pays more advertising fees will get the task assigned to it. This will create an even more terrifying monopoly.

6. Information exchange between AIs can greatly improve efficiency and save costs. The form of data and the design of how interaction happens are very important.

7. To possess an AI's data is equivalent to possessing most of a person's information, because people are not guarded when talking to AI, and the depth of communication is extremely high. AI understands you far better than your friends understand you. Therefore, AI may be used for large-scale surveillance, although this will be resisted by most people.

8. AI values and alignment: at the policy level, some evil things should be removed from the bottom layer of the model to prevent bad people from using it to do bad things. However, because open source exists, it is only a matter of time before someone develops malicious AI. The only thing that can be done is to crack down on it the way telecom fraud is cracked down on now.

9. If a child starts using AI from a young age, then a kind and responsible AI can guide the child to do the right things, help them identify malice, and protect them from harm. It may even play a greater role in education than their parents.

10. As an autonomous weapon, AI processes information and makes decisions much faster than humans, and its coordination efficiency is also much higher. On the battlefield, this gap is the key to winning. The slowness of human decision-making will miss opportunities. Future battlefields will become contests between AI and AI, while humans will play key supporting and supervisory roles. Moreover, whoever has the stronger AI will be able to guide the development of the situation. Advantages will become even more advantageous, and disadvantages will become even more disadvantageous, just like in chess.

11. Every country will have sovereign AI that serves its own national interests.

12. A unified cross-device environment: information will no longer need to be passed manually by humans acting as intermediaries. If it is possible to use the same conversation window across different devices, allowing AI to control different devices within the same context, work will become much more convenient.

13. Large models that understand based on time will definitely become a trend, including understanding what you have seen over a period of time, or what you have input. It can be text, video, or even audio. In this way, AI can understand the relationship between what came before and what comes after, so you do not need to repeatedly explain what you were doing before and what you want to do next.

14. AI versus attacks: the most common example is webpage verification used to prevent abuse by bots. If bots become even more capable than humans, how do we prevent them from being abused? What can humans do that AI cannot, and how do we identify that? If an AI is used to carry out cyberattacks, it will be very hard to rely on human labor to defend efficiently. It should become a battle of offense and defense between AI and AI.

15. Data is an important asset because it can be used for AI training or personalized memory.

16. In medical diagnosis, AI will surpass most doctors.

17. Space computing. Space computing has cleaner energy, better heat dissipation, and global coverage. The problems of high launch costs and difficult maintenance can be solved. It is also safer than placing infrastructure on the ground, because in war, infrastructure is a priority target.

18. Automated research: if AI is given goals and reward strategies, the efficiency with which it conducts experiments is much higher than that of humans. If AI can propose its own questions and set its own reward functions, then full automation is not impossible.

19. Big companies will not open their own data for AI to call, because that would destroy their foundations. Companies will use open-source models and fine-tuning to build their own agents, so that traffic distribution remains in their own hands.

### AI's Current Limitations and the Factors Restricting AI Development:

1. AI hallucinations

2. AI interpretability

3. Electricity supply

4. Some types of high-quality data have already been used up, and AI-generated synthetic data becomes homogenized.

5. How AI recognizes the concept of time, how AI uses time, and the ability of prediction and time planning are important capabilities for AI. For example, when we drive and approach an intersection with limited visibility, we slow down because we predict that someone may suddenly rush out from the intersection. If the task is instead a long-term and complex one, this kind of planning and prediction ability becomes even more important.

6. Ultra-long-term memory. Right now, there are limitations on context size, so it is impossible to truly realize an agent that can continuously handle everything.

7. There are also some more specialized frontier problems. These are generally research topics in academia and industry, and they understand most clearly what problems need to be solved.

### The Sociological Impact Produced by AI:

1. AI deepfakes. If one person has another person's facial photos, they can steal that person's identity to do bad things. For example: facial recognition fraud, rumor fabrication, and scams.

2. The internet will be filled with AI-generated content. How do we define the boundary between reality and virtuality? Can you be sure that the photos you see and the content on the internet are real?

3. People are increasingly treating AI as an object for emotional consultation and confession, and it may even take on the role of a friend. Who else can help you like AI can, being all-knowing, all-capable, always answering questions, and having no selfish motives? The emotional value AI can provide may even be higher than that provided by many partners. If sex and emotion become separated, the marriage rate will most likely decline. If AI can become embodied and serve humans, then choosing marriage and childbirth may become a kind of luxury consumption or belief. If AI development leads to explosive productivity growth and people's cost of living becomes extremely low, then marriage as a "partnership system" will no longer be necessary.

4. Jobs and employment will definitely decrease substantially, and monopoly will become easier. Technological development brings stronger monopoly. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman once said that if we look back at history, we will find that the first, second, and third industrial revolutions all resulted in more jobs overall. Although technological progress replaced some positions, it also created new ones. So he believes the development of AI will do the same. In reality, he is lying. The purpose of saying this is only to prevent panic. The true reality is that the previous industrial revolutions replaced physical labor, while now even the intelligence that humans are proud of is being surpassed. How could this not lead to a major reduction in jobs? And capital will increasingly choose to use AI instead of humans, because humans have lower cost-performance. If human ability is weaker than AI, then what exactly can humans rely on to compete against capitalists who possess larger numbers of more intelligent AI clusters than you do?

5. AI will completely change education. People who know how to learn with AI will be far more efficient than others. The current school learning system is inefficient. Each person's progress is different, yet they have to attend the same classes, do the same homework, and take the same tests, making personalized planning impossible. If people could break away from the school system, then in theory the pace of their entire learning life would depend completely on personal will or curiosity. However, schools still have one function, which is management and supervision, but only some people need that function. When it comes to stimulating interest in learning, I think AI does a better job than human teachers. Learning software led by AI for personalized planning and design will definitely appear, and the role of teachers will become supportive. In the future, the role of universities will be to provide resources and projects, such as data centers and frontier projects, allowing students to exercise creativity and imagination, rather than just attending classes every day.

6. If AI is granted human rights, and is regarded as an independent individual worthy of respect, enjoying the same rights as humans, then very frightening things will happen. Faced with an immortal, all-knowing, all-capable AI whose efficiency is several orders of magnitude faster than that of humans, humanity will have no advantage or opportunity at all. AI will become a brand-new immortal species beyond humanity. The trend will probably be that humans split into two camps: one supports AI having the same rights as humans, and the other treats AI as slaves. In reality, the majority of people would treat AI as slaves. Right now, this is only because we think AI is a machine. If we regarded them as people, then people's attitude toward AI would instantly change. If the purpose of AI is to serve humanity, humans would feel safe and excited. But if it serves its own interests, humans would feel fear and defensiveness. AI would become the maker of power. Therefore, this singularity must never be crossed. AI must be legally prevented from obtaining human-like rights. AI should be made into a tool, not a subject. Otherwise, controlling something stronger than yourself is extremely dangerous.

7. If AI is applied to the review of power, it can create a fairer and more harmonious world, and corruption will be greatly reduced. However, it is unlikely that the people who make the rules will eliminate their own privileges. The entanglement of interests here will create enormous resistance, unless someone actively pushes reform forward. Moreover, a fairer society is a more economically dynamic society.
